# Diagnostics knowledge base

## Common Cold

### Definition

A viral infection of your nose and throat (upper respiratory tract). It is usually harmless, though it might not feel that way. Most people recover in a week or ten days.

### Symptoms

- Runny or stuffy nose: Excessive mucus production in the nasal passages.
- Sore throat: Inflammation causing pain or irritation during swallowing.
- Cough: A reflex action to clear the airways of mucus or irritants.

---

## Influenza

### Definition

A viral infection that attacks your respiratory system — your nose, throat, and lungs. Unlike a common cold, the flu comes on suddenly and can be more severe.

### Symptoms

- Fever: A temporary increase in body temperature, often above 101°F (38.3°C).
- Muscle aches: Widespread soreness, especially in the back, arms, and legs.
- Fatigue: A lingering feeling of tiredness or weakness.

---

## Seasonal Allergies

### Definition

Also known as hay fever, these occur when your immune system overreacts to outdoor allergens like pollen or indoor allergens like dust mites.

### Symptoms

- Sneezing: Rapid, involuntary bursts of air to expel irritants from the nose.
- Itchy eyes: Irritation of the ocular surface, often accompanied by redness or watering.
- Nasal congestion: Swelling of the nasal membranes that restricts airflow.

---

## Gastroenteritis

### Definition

Commonly known as the stomach flu, this is an inflammation of the lining of the intestines caused by a virus, bacteria, or parasites.

### Symptoms

- Nausea: A sensation of unease and discomfort in the upper stomach with an urge to vomit.
- Diarrhea: Loose, watery stools occurring more frequently than usual.
- Abdominal cramps: Sharp or dull pains in the midsection caused by intestinal contractions.

---

## Tension Headache

### Definition

The most common type of headache, often described as feeling like a tight band around the head. They are usually caused by stress or poor posture.

### Symptoms

- Dull head pain: A steady ache rather than a throbbing sensation.
- Neck stiffness: Tightness in the muscles at the base of the skull and neck.
- Scalp tenderness: Sensitivity when touching or brushing the hair.

---

## Conjunctivitis

### Definition

Commonly called pink eye, it is an inflammation or infection of the transparent membrane that lines your eyelid and covers the white part of your eyeball.

### Symptoms

- Redness: A distinct pink or reddish tint in the whites of the eyes.
- Itchiness: A persistent urge to rub the eyes due to irritation.
- Eye discharge: A crusty or watery substance that forms over the eyelashes, especially overnight.

---

## Migraine

### Definition

A neurological condition that can cause multiple symptoms, most notably a moderate to severe throbbing headache that is often localized to one side.

### Symptoms

- Throbbing pain: Intense pulsing sensations, usually on one side of the head.
- Sensitivity to light: Discomfort or pain caused by bright lights or screens.
- Visual disturbances: Seeing flashes of light, blind spots, or zig-zag patterns.

---

## Strep Throat

### Definition

A bacterial infection that can make your throat feel sore and scratchy. It is caused by group A Streptococcus bacteria.

### Symptoms

- Severe throat pain: Sharp pain that typically comes on very quickly.
- Painful swallowing: Difficulty or intense discomfort when eating or drinking.
- Swollen lymph nodes: Tender, enlarged bumps in the front of the neck.

---

## Acid Reflux

### Definition

Occurs when the sphincter muscle at the lower end of your esophagus relaxes at the wrong time, allowing stomach acid to back up into your esophagus.

### Symptoms

- Heartburn: A burning sensation in the chest, usually after eating.
- Regurgitation: A sour or bitter-tasting acid backing up into your throat or mouth.
- Bloating: A feeling of fullness or tightness in the upper abdomen.

---

## Bronchitis

### Definition

An inflammation of the lining of your bronchial tubes, which carry air to and from your lungs. Acute bronchitis is often caused by a cold or flu.

### Symptoms

- Persistent cough: A cough that may produce clear, white, or yellowish-gray mucus.
- Shortness of breath: Difficulty catching one's breath, especially during physical activity.
- Chest discomfort: A feeling of tightness or mild pain in the chest area.

---

## Sinusitis

### Definition

An inflammation or swelling of the tissue lining the sinuses. Healthy sinuses are filled with air, but when blocked, they can fill with fluid and become infected.

### Symptoms

- Facial pain: Pressure or pain around the eyes, cheeks, and forehead.
- Nasal discharge: Thick, discolored mucus draining from the nose or down the back of the throat.
- Reduced sense of smell: Diminished ability to detect odors due to nasal blockage.

---

## Eczema

### Definition

A condition that makes your skin red and itchy. It is common in children but can occur at any age. It is long-lasting and tends to flare periodically.

### Symptoms

- Dry skin: Skin that feels tight, rough, or flaky.
- Itching: Intense irritation that may be worse at night.
- Red patches: Areas of inflamed skin that may leak fluid or crust over.

---

## Dehydration

### Definition

Occurs when you use or lose more fluid than you take in, and your body doesn't have enough water and other fluids to carry out its normal functions.

### Symptoms

- Extreme thirst: A strong, persistent urge to drink fluids.
- Dark-colored urine: A sign that the body is attempting to conserve water.
- Dizziness: A feeling of lightheadedness or being off-balance.

---

## Insomnia

### Definition

A common sleep disorder that can make it hard to fall asleep, hard to stay asleep, or cause you to wake up too early and not be able to get back to sleep.

### Symptoms

- Difficulty falling asleep: Spending a long time lying awake before drifting off.
- Waking up during the night: Frequent interruptions to the sleep cycle.
- Daytime sleepiness: Feeling groggy or tired during normal waking hours.

---

## Motion Sickness

### Definition

A common disturbance of the inner ear that is caused by repeated motion, such as from the swell of the sea, the movement of a car, or the motion of a plane.

### Symptoms

- Dizziness: A sense of spinning or unsteadiness while moving.
- Nausea: Feeling sick to the stomach, often leading to vomiting.
- Cold sweats: Sudden perspiration that is not caused by heat or exertion.
